// CONDENSATION REACTIONS / 01

The Cannizzaro Reaction

A disproportionation reaction in which an aldehyde with no alpha-hydrogen simultaneously acts as both oxidising and reducing agent in the presence of concentrated alkali. One molecule is oxidised to the carboxylate, the other is reduced to the alcohol.

// KEY NOTE

Industrial Applications

Where the Cannizzaro reaction matters beyond the flask.

// APPLICATION 01

Pentaerythritol synthesis

4 HCHO + CH₃CHO → C(CH₂OH)₄

Pentaerythritol is made by reacting four equivalents of formaldehyde with acetaldehyde under base — three equivalents undergo aldol addition with acetaldehyde to install three CH₂OH groups, then the fourth equivalent undergoes a crossed Cannizzaro with the resulting aldehyde to reduce it to the fourth CH₂OH and oxidise HCHO to formate. The product C(CH₂OH)₄ is used in explosives (PETN), alkyd resins, and as a medicine for angina.

// APPLICATION 02

Benzyl alcohol production

2 C₆H₅CHO → C₆H₅CH₂OH + C₆H₅COO⁻

Benzaldehyde undergoes self-Cannizzaro in concentrated NaOH to give equimolar amounts of benzyl alcohol and sodium benzoate. Benzyl alcohol is used in perfumery, as a solvent in inks and paints, and as a preservative in injectable drugs. Sodium benzoate is a food preservative (E211).

// APPLICATION 03

Formaldehyde disproportionation

Industrial formate and methanol production

Formaldehyde is the most reactive substrate for the Cannizzaro reaction because it has no substituents to hinder hydride transfer. Self-Cannizzaro of HCHO gives methanol and formate. This has been used industrially as a route to methanol and sodium formate from formaldehyde and NaOH under controlled conditions.

// CONDENSATION REACTIONS / 02

The Perkin Condensation

An aromatic aldehyde condenses with an acid anhydride in the presence of the sodium salt of the corresponding acid. The alpha-carbon of the anhydride acts as the nucleophile. The product is an alpha,beta-unsaturated acid — classically cinnamic acid from benzaldehyde and acetic anhydride.

Industrial Applications

The Perkin condensation in synthesis and industry.

// APPLICATION 01

Cinnamic acid synthesis

C₆H₅CHO + (CH₃CO)₂O → C₆H₅CH=CHCOOH

The most classic Perkin reaction. Benzaldehyde, acetic anhydride, and sodium acetate at 180°C give cinnamic acid (trans-3-phenylpropenoic acid). Used in perfumery, as a precursor to cinnamaldehyde, and in the synthesis of amino acids including phenylalanine by Erlenmeyer synthesis.

// APPLICATION 02

Coumarin synthesis

2-hydroxybenzaldehyde + (CH₃CO)₂O → coumarin

When salicylaldehyde (2-hydroxybenzaldehyde) is used in the Perkin condensation, the initially formed cinnamic acid derivative undergoes spontaneous lactonisation — the phenolic OH attacks the double bond in a ring-closing step to give coumarin. Used in perfumery and as the parent compound of many pharmaceutical anticoagulants (warfarin family).

// CONDENSATION REACTIONS / 03

The Claisen Condensation

Two ester molecules condense in the presence of a strong base (sodium ethoxide) to give a beta-ketoester. One ester loses its alpha-hydrogen to form an enolate, which then attacks the carbonyl of the second ester. The alkoxide leaving group departs and the beta-ketoester product is formed.

// KEY NOTE

Industrial Applications

The Claisen condensation as a synthetic workhorse.

// APPLICATION 01

Ethyl acetoacetate

2 CH₃COOEt + NaOEt → CH₃COCH₂COOEt

Self-Claisen condensation of ethyl acetate gives ethyl acetoacetate (acetoacetic ester), one of the most important synthetic intermediates in organic chemistry. Used in the acetoacetic ester synthesis to make ketones and monosubstituted acetic acids via alkylation and controlled hydrolysis/decarboxylation.

// APPLICATION 02

Dimedone (via Dieckmann)

Cyclic beta-ketoester from diethyl pimelate

Diethyl pimelate undergoes Dieckmann cyclisation — intramolecular Claisen — in sodium ethoxide to give the cyclic beta-ketoester, which after hydrolysis and decarboxylation gives dimedone (5,5-dimethylcyclohexane-1,3-dione). Dimedone is widely used in analytical chemistry to characterise aldehydes.

// APPLICATION 03

Natural product synthesis

Beta-ketoesters as building blocks

Beta-ketoesters produced by Claisen condensation are versatile building blocks. Their active methylene group (between two carbonyls) has a pKa of ~11, making it easy to alkylate. This has been exploited in the synthesis of terpenes, steroids, and many pharmaceutical targets starting from simple ester starting materials.

// CONDENSATION REACTIONS / 04

The Benzoin Condensation

Two molecules of benzaldehyde condense in the presence of cyanide ion (or an N-heterocyclic carbene) to give benzoin — an alpha-hydroxy ketone. The cyanide acts as a catalyst by temporarily reversing the polarity of the carbonyl carbon, a concept called umpolung.

// KEY NOTE

Industrial Applications

Benzoin and its derivatives in synthesis and material science.

// APPLICATION 01

Photoinitiators

Benzoin ethers in UV-curing polymers

Benzoin and its alkyl ethers are classic photoinitiators for radical polymerisation. On UV irradiation, the C-C bond alpha to the carbonyl undergoes homolytic cleavage to generate two radicals that initiate chain polymerisation. Used in UV-curable coatings, inks, dental composites, and 3D printing resins.

// APPLICATION 02

Benzil and benzilate rearrangement

Benzoin → benzil → benzilic acid

Benzoin is oxidised to benzil (C₆H₅-CO-CO-C₆H₅) by nitric acid or copper acetate. Benzil then undergoes the benzilic acid rearrangement in strong base — a 1,2-aryl shift to give benzilic acid (C₆H₅)₂C(OH)COOH. This is the first example of a 1,2-aryl shift in chemistry and benzilic acid is used in the synthesis of glycopyrrolate.

// APPLICATION 03

Umpolung strategy in synthesis

NHC-catalysed crossed benzoin for complex targets

The NHC-catalysed version of the benzoin condensation allows crossed benzoin reactions with high selectivity and enantioselectivity using chiral NHC precatalysts. This umpolung strategy has been applied in the total synthesis of complex natural products including plakortide and several polyketide natural products.
